channel-capacity

Shannon capacity-achieving prior uniqueness in Lean 4.

paperlean 4information theory  ·  April 2026  ·  repository

channel-capacity is a Lean 4 library that formalizes Shannon capacity-achieving prior uniqueness for Markov kernels. For a Markov kernel k : α ⇝ β between measurable spaces, Shannon's channel capacity is the supremum of the mutual information I(p, k) taken over probability measures on the input alphabet. Three classical questions follow: when is the supremum attained, when is the attaining input unique, and what structural hypothesis on k is both sufficient and honest? The library gives three aligned answers.

Three layers

In the finite-alphabet setting the answer is textbook. The input simplex is compact, the mutual-information functional is continuous and concave, and uniqueness holds whenever the channel is structurally non-degenerate enough to rule out two distinct inputs inducing the same output distribution. In the general measure-theoretic setting the picture is less uniform: compactness of P(α), continuity or upper semicontinuity of p ↦ I(p, k) in a useful topology, and the right analogue of "full-rank rows" each require work, and different regularity packages produce different theorems.

The library brackets the answer with three layers:

  • A finite-alphabet theorem with compactness, continuity, and strict concavity discharged internally on Mathlib's canonical weak topology for ProbabilityMeasure.
  • A concrete discharged theorem for compact-Polish channels with a common strictly positive jointly continuous density against a finite reference measure.
  • A broader measure-theoretic packaging theorem that isolates the abstract hypotheses needed for future upstream generalization.

Lean v4.29.1, Mathlib only, warning-free build, zero sorry / admit / axiom.

The finite theorem

The cleanest statement in the library. Its signature uses only Mathlib vocabulary and the proof depends only on Mathlib-native lemmas, which is why it is the natural upstream candidate. 

theorem exists_unique_capacity_achieving_prior_of_finite
    {α β : Type*}
    [Fintype α] [Finite β]
    [MeasurableSpace α] [MeasurableSpace β]
    [MeasurableSingletonClass α] [MeasurableSingletonClass β]
    [Nonempty α]
    (k : Kernel α β) [IsMarkovKernel k]
    (h : Kernel.RowMatrixFullRank k) :
    ∃! p : ProbabilityMeasure α, mutualInformation p k = channelCapacity k

The only non-degeneracy hypothesis is that the real linear map induced by the kernel's row matrix is injective. No topology, compactness, semicontinuity, or abstract bundle hypothesis appears in the statement: they are all discharged inside the proof. The assembly uses four Mathlib-native ingredients — a finite entropy formula that reduces mutual information to the discrete Shannon expression, strict concavity of output entropy via Real.strictConcaveOn_negMulLog, compactness of ProbabilityMeasure α via MeasureTheory.Measure.Prokhorov, and continuity of p ↦ I(p, k) — plus one bridge lemma converting row-matrix full rank into injectivity of the prior pushforward.

The compact-Polish theorem

Real channel models are rarely finite, and most tractable continuous channel models come with a positive continuous density against a natural reference measure. The discharged theorem packages that regularity as a structure class Kernel.ContinuousPositiveDensity — all rows share a strictly positive jointly continuous density against a finite reference ν — and proves uniqueness on compact-Polish alphabets. 

theorem exists_unique_capacity_achieving_prior_discharged
    (k : Kernel α β) [IsMarkovKernel k]
    (ν : Measure β) [IsFiniteMeasure ν]
    (hDensity : Kernel.ContinuousPositiveDensity k ν)
    (hInj : Kernel.InjectivePriorPushforward k) :
    ∃! p : ProbabilityMeasure α, mutualInformation p k = channelCapacity k

Four of the five ContinuousPositiveDensity fields are discharged from density data: absolute continuity of rows, uniform density bounds on the compact product (producing a reference KL bound via the elementary inequality x log x + 1 − x ≤ x² + 1), finiteness of the reference KL, and the chain rule. The fifth — upper semicontinuity of the mutual-information functional — is carried rather than discharged. For finite alphabets this is populated mechanically from the finite continuity result; for general compact-Polish alphabets with a positive continuous density, a derived theorem has not yet been proved. The library calls this out explicitly so users of the discharged theorem are not misled.

The general packaging

The packaging theorem exists_unique_capacity_achieving_prior takes an injectivity hypothesis, a WellConditionedForCapacity bundle, nonemptiness and compactness of P(α), and upper semicontinuity of the mutual-information functional, and returns existence-and-uniqueness. Users with their own regularity framework — a different density class, a topological input constraint, bounded-information hypotheses — can apply it directly and supply their own discharge for the abstract hypotheses without coupling their work to ContinuousPositiveDensity. The compact-Polish theorem is then an instance of how to use it.

Sharpness: row separation is not enough

The library includes an opt-in Fin 6 → Fin 3 permutation-channel counterexample whose rows are the six permutations of (1/2, 1/3, 1/6). The file proves that all six rows are pairwise distinct as probability measures on the output alphabet (RowSeparating), but that the prior pushforward map is not injective — the prior uniformly supported on {0, 3, 4} and the prior uniformly supported on {1, 2, 5} both push forward to the uniform distribution on {0, 1, 2}.

This is the load-bearing sharpness content. A naive reading of "full-rank rows" in the measure-theoretic setting would try RowSeparating. The counterexample shows this is strictly weaker than InjectivePriorPushforward: pairwise-distinct rows can admit distinct priors with the same output marginal. The uniqueness theorems need the stronger form, and no uniqueness result along these lines can be stated at the weaker hypothesis.

Why three layers

The layered structure is deliberate. The finite theorem exists to be upstream-ready: clean Mathlib vocabulary, a single linear-algebra hypothesis, no bundle machinery in the signature. The compact-Polish theorem exists to be the strongest concrete measure-theoretic result: the minimum structural bundle under which the absolute-continuity and chain-rule machinery close, honestly labeled with the one field still carried. The general theorem exists as the packaging: an abstract interface that other regularity frameworks can specialize without adopting ContinuousPositiveDensity.

The motivating application is a Shannon-correspondence for governance channels in the legitimacy framework; that development consumes this library as a dependency and is documented in its own paper. The present library stands on its own information-theoretic terms.
