legitimacy

Impossibility, machine-checked governance, and the Shannon correspondence.

Mainstream AI "governance" in 2026 is a runtime-enforcement plane: rate limits, PII guards, prompt-injection detection, audit logs. Necessary work, but upstream of an unasked question — is the rule being enforced actually coherent?

The legitimacy thread pursues that question across three layers: an impossibility theorem in the Arrow lineage, a machine-checked constitutional kernel that makes the theorem executable, and a Shannon-correspondence companion library connecting governance channels to information-theoretic uniqueness results.

The impossibility

No peer-relative governance system can jointly satisfy consistency, solidarity, monotonicity, and strategyproofness. Every deployed system sacrifices at least one — typically without declaring which, bounding how much, or specifying a monitor for the sacrificed axiom. The impossibility theorem and its Young-style graph diagnostics make that sacrifice explicit and checkable.

rustlean 4 legitimacy
machine-checked constitutional kernel
A Rust crate that extracts governance graphs from source or policy files and audits them against the five-axiom kernel (Certifiable, Governance-observable, Corrigible, Compositional Safety, Non-Vacuous). Backed by a Lean 4 / Mathlib library with hundreds of machine-verified declarations and zero sorry. Orthogonal to gateway products — they enforce; this audits.
lean 4 channel-capacity
Shannon-correspondence companion library
Three layered uniqueness theorems for capacity-achieving input priors on Markov kernels: a finite-alphabet result, a compact-Polish discharged result, and a measure-theoretic packaging result. legitimacy consumes this library to connect governance channels to information-theoretic uniqueness. See the writing entry for the mathematical exposition.

Current state

The Rust crate and Lean library are in active development on a private repository and will be opened on publication of Paper 1. The channel-capacity companion library is public and stable. The paper itself is in preparation.
